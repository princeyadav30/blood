Why Donate Blood?

Blood donation is a simple, safe way to make a big difference in the lives of others. Here's why your donation matters:

Save Lives

One blood donation can save up to three lives. Your contribution could help accident victims, surgery patients, newborns, and those battling cancer.

Health Benefits

Regular blood donation helps reduce harmful iron stores, lowers risk of heart disease, and stimulates production of new blood cells.

How Blood Donation Works

The donation process is simple, safe, and takes less than an hour from start to finish.

1

Registration

Complete our quick registration form with your basic information and medical history.

2

Health Screening

A trained professional will check your temperature, pulse, blood pressure and hemoglobin level.

3

Donation

The actual donation takes about 8-10 minutes during which approximately 1 pint of blood is collected.

4

Refreshment

Enjoy snacks and drinks for 10-15 minutes before resuming your day. Your body replaces the fluid within hours.

Donor Eligibility

To ensure the safety of both donors and recipients, these are our general eligibility requirements:

Basic Requirements

  • Be at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent)
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds (50 kg)
  • Be in good general health
  • Have valid ID with photo and birth date

Donation Frequency

  • Whole blood: Every 56 days (up to 6 times/year)
  • Platelets: Every 7 days (up to 24 times/year)
  • Plasma: Every 28 days (up to 13 times/year)
  • Double red cells: Every 112 days (up to 3 times/year)

Medical Considerations

  • No cold or flu symptoms for 48 hours
  • No antibiotics for 24 hours
  • No major surgery in last 6 months
  • No tattoos/piercings in last 4 months

Temporary Deferrals

  • Pregnancy: 6 weeks postpartum
  • Travel to malaria-risk areas: 3 months
  • Dental procedures: 24-72 hours
  • Certain vaccinations: 2-4 weeks

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about blood donation

Is donating blood safe?

Absolutely. All equipment is sterile, used only once, and then discarded. There's no risk of contracting any infectious disease from donating blood. Our staff are highly trained professionals who follow strict safety protocols.

How long does the process take?

The entire process takes about an hour from start to finish. The actual blood donation only takes 8-10 minutes. This includes registration, a health screening, donation time, and refreshments afterward.

How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (about 8 weeks). Platelet donations can be made more frequently - up to 24 times per year. Your body replaces the donated fluid within 24 hours and red blood cells within 4-6 weeks.

What should I do before donating?

Eat a healthy meal and drink plenty of fluids before your donation. Avoid fatty foods. Get a good night's sleep. Bring your ID and list of any medications you're taking. Wear comfortable clothing with sleeves that can be rolled up.

What happens to my donated blood?

Your donation is tested, processed, and distributed to hospitals where it's needed most. Whole blood can be separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets to help multiple patients. You'll receive notification when your blood is used to help someone.
